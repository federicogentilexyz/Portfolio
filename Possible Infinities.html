“Possible Infinities” is a site specific sound installation created by artist Pier Alfeo and designed with my contribution on all aspects of 3D modeling. Set in the historic garden of Carlo Scarpa at Fondazione Querini Stampalia in Venice the sculpture is the second of Pier Alfeo’s series “Biomimesi” and it takes inspiration from Luigi Nono’s composition “A Carlo Scarpa, architetto, ai suoi infiniti possibili”. At the heart of the project lies MCDSBA (Markov Chain Driven Structural Blooming Algorithms), an innovative framework co-developed by Pier Alfeo and Francesco Vitucci at the Conservatorio “N. Piccinni” in Bari. This system converts musical scores into three-dimensional graphs, generating new soundscapes that preserve the spirit of the original compositions while evolving into autonomous, living sonic forms.

It features a composition reproduced by a multi-channel speaker network and the main structure was realized by designing a system composed of metal tubular poles and 3D printed elements.

“Possible Infinities” consists of a hybrid sound sculpture derived from computational analyses. A sensitive technological organism that breathes and resonates like a living system. It recalls the “spikemoss” plant, a desert species capable of returning to life after dormancy, symbolizing perpetual cycles of rebirth and transformation.

Presented at Fondazione Querini Stampalia in Venice in October 2025.
