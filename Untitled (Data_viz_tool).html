A lonely server gazes into the web flow. Every request, every loop of doomscrolling, every invisible exchange is analyzed by comparing the energy cost of digital life with the biomass yield of a single miscanthus giganteus plant. A report on the environmental costs of invisible actions that leave a trace in data, power, and carbon emissions. A network rooted in soil rather than silicon.

Untitled (Data_visualization_tool) is a visual simulation inspired by the interface of a system terminal. The work displays in real time a series of data related to the energy consumption of everyday digital activities (e.g., streaming, social media, online communication) compared to the bioenergetic yield of miscanthus giganteus, a high-efficiency energy crop cultivated for biomass production.

The interface generates animated strings that mimic the language of network logs or technical diagnostic systems, along with infographics that represent data on the plant and on the countries with the largest miscanthus harvests. Each line corresponds to a digital action or to an agricultural or environmental parameter, offering a direct comparison between immaterial infrastructures and biological resources.

The work proposes a way to interpret the environmental impact of our online actions, in which the plant becomes a concrete unit of measurement for CO₂ emissions, watt-hours, and water consumption.
