Materials Poplar wood, industrial belt, electronics

Detroit is a compact and modular home hi-fi system designed to deliver high-fidelity audio playback, inspired by sound systems and scoop bin speakers used to amplify low frequencies. The system consists of a wooden cubic speaker and a series of modular panels that allow users to customize the assembly of the speakers according to their aesthetic and functional preferences.

The scoop bin, a key element of the system, is designed for easy assembly through a simple interlocking mechanism that requires no special tools or advanced technical skills. This ease of assembly enables anyone to set up or dismantle the component effortlessly, adapting the system to the needs of the moment.

Once assembled, the system is securely held together by a fastening strap, which not only provides practical stability and safety but also serves as an aesthetic detail. This strap recalls the construction and transport methods of traditional sound systems, often quickly assembled and disassembled for itinerant events and concerts. It gives the system a robust and dynamic appearance while evoking the culture of sound systems, where modularity and portability are central.
