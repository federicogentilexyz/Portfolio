Federico Gentile
Works About Contacts
Year 2025
Type Audiovisual installation
Dimensions Sculpture (40x133x40cm), installation (variable)
Materials Polystyrene, jesmonite, paint, moss, pine needles, laser pointer, A/V installation: real time generation made with touchdesigner

“Monolith” was born from my fascination with technology as an entity produced by nature. I have always found it incredible that, thanks to just a couple of rocks, I can make a video call to the other side of the world today. At the same time, I find it equally intriguing that this very video call is contributing to the destruction of the nature that enabled it.

The installation presents itself as a confrontation between the accelerationism of urban civilization and the static nature of the natural world. Nature requires a reason to exist, and it finds this reason in an eternal conflict with an entity that originates from herself, a “non-self”, which then attempts to fight her: the machine, the digital.

The monolith, representing the immobility of nature, is an unmovable element with an immense geological weight. Within the installation, it is continuously carved away by a laser, a technological derivative of itself, that tries to erode, damage, and ultimately replace it. Similarly, the photogrammetries within the space serve as a flawed representation of nature. The machine attempts to reach the perfection of its creator. It generates, recalculates and erases, yet it never achieves its goal, trapped in a continuous and frustrating struggle to replace its own origin.

“Monolith” is an eternal conflict that must take place for the two entities involved, nature and technology, to have a reason to exist.

Presented at Fondazione Bevilacqua La Masa in Venice in February 2025.
